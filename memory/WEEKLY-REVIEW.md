### What Worked
- **Capital fully preserved.** Equity flat to the cent ($99,681.72) through a transitional, two-sided tape — zero drawdown this week.
- **Discipline held through FOMC day (Wed 6/17).** Correctly stayed in cash ahead of and through the rate decision rather than forcing a position into event risk.
- **Buy gate did its job.** Every candidate screened (CAT, DE, JPM) was correctly rejected on volume/breakout failures — none had 1.5x volume confirmation or a clean 3–8% pullback; the deterministic gate even caught a garbled WebSearch breakout figure on DE.
- **Regime flip detected promptly.** The OFF→ON transition was identified at the 6/17 pre-market (SPY 750.58 > 20-day SMA 746.43, cross-confirmed on Gemini) and a Discord alert was sent.
- **No forced trades.** Patience over activity — a 100%-cash week is a valid outcome when nothing clears the gate.

### What Didn't Work
- **Lagged the S&P (+0.93%) by sitting in cash** — real opportunity cost, though justified by the absence of any gate-passing setup.
- **Unresolved documentation gap carried in from last week.** The CVX exit (~6/12, ~$180.77, ~-4.63%) still has no committed research/trade-log entry and remains operator-unconfirmed.
- **No setup capitalized on even after regime flipped ON Wed** — correct given the gate failures, but the watchlist produced nothing actionable.
- **Earlier daily-snapshot gaps** (6/05, 6/08 previously noted) underscore that routine cadence has been inconsistent.

### Key Lessons
- A 100%-cash week is the right answer when no candidate clears the 11-check gate — preserving capital through a regime transition and an FOMC print beats chasing extended breakouts.
- Regime ON is permission, not a signal: still need a volume-confirmed breakout *plus* a 3–8% pullback before any order.
- Close the loop on the CVX exit — undocumented fills erode the integrity of the trade log and must be reconciled with the operator.
